The Science Behind PassBoard

Why PassBoard Works
When Flashcards Don't

Most students study by rereading notes until they feel familiar. Familiarity is not memory. PassBoard is built around six evidence-based learning techniques — the same methods used by top medical and bar exam programs.

1

Retrieval Practice

Answering questions forces active recall — building 2× stronger memory than passive rereading.

Roediger & Butler, 2011 — Perspectives on Psychological Science
2

Spaced Repetition

Study schedule spaces sessions to catch information right before it fades — the most efficient study method known to science.

Cepeda et al., 2006 — Psychological Science
3

Elaborative Interrogation

Every explanation answers "why" — because understanding why something is true makes it 2-3× more memorable than memorizing it.

Pressley et al., 1992
4

Dual Coding

Real-world analogies pair verbal explanations with visual mental images — two pathways to every piece of information.

Paivio, 1991
5

Interleaving

Questions from different topics are mixed, matching PSI exam conditions and eliminating "topic tunnel vision."

Kornell & Bjork, 2008 — Psychological Science
6

Metacognitive Monitoring

Topic mastery ratings show exactly what you know vs. don't know — so you study what matters, not what feels comfortable.

Dunlosky et al., 2013 — Psych Science in the Public Interest
